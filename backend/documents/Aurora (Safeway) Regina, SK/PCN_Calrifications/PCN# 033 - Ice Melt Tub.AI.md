## Executive Summary

This PCN eliminates the previously specified ice melt tub installation from the project scope. The stainless steel ice melt station/tub was to be owner-supplied and installed by the contractor, but is now being deleted entirely. The concrete tub area will instead be finished with epoxy flooring to match adjacent surfaces.

## 5. Code Compliance Notes

### Accessibility
- **Floor Surface**: Epoxy floor must be slip-resistant (commercial kitchen requirement)
- **Level Changes**: If tub creates floor depression, may require:
  - Infill to maintain level floor
  - Or compliant slope transitions
  - Visual contrast if level change >1/4"

### Plumbing Code
- **Drainage**: Ice machines typically require:
  - Indirect waste connection
  - Air gap for sanitation
  - If ice melt tub provided this, alternative drainage method needed
  - Floor sink or alternative receptor may be required

### Food Safety
- **Sanitation**: Ice handling area requires:
  - Easily cleanable surfaces
  - Proper drainage
  - No standing water
  - Epoxy floor finish addresses cleanability

### Building Code
- **Floor Finishes**: Commercial food preparation areas
  - Non-absorbent surfaces required
  - Epoxy FF3 likely complies
  - Must be seamless and cove base at walls

## Summary

PCN #033 removes a stainless steel ice melt tub from the Seafood Department scope. The deletion affects architectural details, equipment schedules, and likely mechanical/plumbing systems. The most critical coordination issue is determining the status of the concrete tub construction and ensuring proper drainage for the ice machine that would have used this tub. All affected tub surfaces will receive epoxy FF3 flooring to match adjacent areas. The contractor must submit pricing before any work proceeds, and the change requires formal Change Order authorization.
