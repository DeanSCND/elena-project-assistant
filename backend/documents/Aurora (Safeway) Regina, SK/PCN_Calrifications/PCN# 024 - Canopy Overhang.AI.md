## Executive Summary

This PCN addresses a coordination issue between structural and architectural elements at the high parapet/canopy overhang. The structural steel overhang extends approximately 16" beyond the exterior ACM (Aluminum Composite Material) cladding, creating an excessive and architecturally unacceptable projection. The resolution requires cutting back steel deck and supports to align with architectural intent and making structural repairs to maintain system integrity.

**Primary Issue**: Structural steel overhang at Grid Line F extends 3'-5" from gridline, while architectural ACM wall is only 1'-1.5" from gridline, resulting in ~16" excessive overhang.

**Resolution**: Cut steel deck and supports back to suit architectural details and repair/reinforce remaining structure per engineering specifications.

## 4. Identified Conflicts

### Primary Conflict: Structural vs. Architectural Coordination

**Nature of Conflict**:
- Structural steel design shows canopy overhang extending 3'-5" beyond Grid Line F
- Architectural design shows exterior ACM wall face at 1'-1.5" beyond Grid Line F
- Results in structural steel protruding approximately 16" beyond finished exterior wall

**Impact**:
- Aesthetically unacceptable - exposed structural steel beyond building envelope
- Weather protection issues - steel deck exposed beyond wall protection
- Potential for water infiltration at connection
- Does not match design intent shown in architectural elevations

### Secondary Conflict: Design Coordination Issue

**Root Cause Analysis**:
- Structural drawings (S2.4/5) show full extension of steel framing to accommodate loads
- Architectural sections (A5.0/3) show reduced wall projection for aesthetic reasons
- Lack of coordination between disciplines during design phase
- Issue discovered during construction when steel was already fabricated/installed

**Timing Issues**:
- Steel likely already fabricated and installed before conflict identified
- RFI submitted July 9, 2025, suggesting issue found during field review
- Requires field modification rather than design correction

### Construction Sequencing Conflict

**Observed Issues**:
- Photos show steel structure already erected
- Modification requires cutting back installed steel members
- Light gauge framing and ACM installation cannot proceed until steel is corrected
- Potential schedule delay for cutting and reinforcing operations

### Structural Integrity Considerations

**Potential Concerns**:
1. **Load Path Modification**:
   - Cutting back steel deck reduces lateral diaphragm area
   - May affect load transfer to vertical elements
   - Engineer has provided reinforcement detail to address

2. **Connection Adequacy**:
   - New trimmed edge requires continuous support track
   - Additional fasteners specified (Kwik Flex and Hilti SDS screws)
   - Edge condition requires careful execution

3. **Cantilever Reduction**:
   - Reducing overhang affects moment conditions at support
   - Original HSS sizing may have accounted for longer cantilever
   - Engineer's detail suggests existing HSS adequate after modification

## 6. Technical Analysis

### Modification Procedure

**Step 1: Layout and Protection**:
- Survey and mark Grid Line F location on installed steel
- Establish cut line per architectural requirements (1'-1.5" beyond Grid F)
- Install temporary bracing if required
- Protect areas below from falling debris

**Step 2: Cutting Operations**:
- Cut steel deck back to suit architectural wall location
- Cut or modify HSS support members as required
- Maintain structural stability during cutting
- Remove cut sections safely

**Step 3: Edge Reinforcement**:
- Install continuous 4" x 18Ga. steel track with 2" flange
- Fasten track to HSS with 1/4-20 Kwik Flex fasteners from above and below
- Trim deck to allow track to fit between deck and HSS
- Fasten deck to track with Hilti #12 SDS screws at 12" O.C.

**Step 4: Verification**:
- Verify all fasteners installed per specification
- Check alignment with architectural wall location
- Photograph completed work for record
- Obtain engineer/inspector approval

### Load Transfer Mechanism

**Original Design**:
- Steel deck spans to HSS outriggers
- Load transfers through deck diaphragm to structural frame
- Cantilever overhang beyond Grid F

**Modified Design**:
- Reduced deck cantilever
- Continuous edge track supports trimmed deck edge
- Track fastened to HSS distributes loads
- Deck fastened to track maintains diaphragm action

### Structural Adequacy

**Engineer's Intent**:
- Modification detail provided by P.Eng. indicates structural adequacy reviewed
- HSS members sized for actual loads, not overhang length
- Edge track provides adequate support for reduced deck span
- Fastener schedule maintains load transfer capacity
